# Expressing Opinions & Debate

Being able to share and defend your point of view — and respond gracefully to others — is a defining B1 skill. This unit gives you the phrases and structures to navigate opinions, agreements, and disagreements naturally in Mexican Spanish.

## Giving Your Opinion

### Stating a position
> **Creo que** la situación va a mejorar. — *I think / I believe the situation will improve.*
> **Pienso que** es una buena idea. — *I think it's a good idea.*
> **En mi opinión**, necesitamos más tiempo. — *In my opinion, we need more time.*
> **Para mí**, lo más importante es la familia. — *For me, the most important thing is family.*
> **Desde mi punto de vista**, el problema es otro. — *From my point of view, the problem is different.*
> **Me parece que** hay una solución mejor. — *It seems to me there's a better solution.*

### Expressing certainty or doubt
> **Estoy seguro/a de que** tiene razón. — *I'm sure that he's right.*
> **No estoy seguro/a, pero** creo que... — *I'm not sure, but I think...*
> **Es posible que** tengamos razón. — *It's possible that we're right.*
> **Puede que** haya otra explicación. — *There may be another explanation.*

## Agreeing

> **Tienes razón.** — *You're right.*
> **Estoy de acuerdo (contigo).** — *I agree (with you).*
> **Exactamente.** — *Exactly.*
> **Así es.** — *That's right. / That's how it is.*
> **Claro que sí.** — *Of course.*
> **Sin duda.** — *Without a doubt.*
> **Yo también pienso eso.** — *I think so too.*

## Partially Agreeing

> **Sí, pero...** — *Yes, but...*
> **Tienes razón en parte, pero...** — *You're partly right, but...*
> **Es verdad, aunque...** — *It's true, although...*
> **Entiendo tu punto, sin embargo...** — *I understand your point, however...*
> **Puede ser, pero también hay que considerar...** — *That may be, but we also need to consider...*

## Disagreeing — Politely

> **No estoy de acuerdo.** — *I don't agree.*
> **Yo lo veo diferente.** — *I see it differently.*
> **No creo que sea así.** — *I don't think that's the case.*
> **Discrepo un poco.** — *I disagree a bit.* (formal)
> **Con todo respeto, creo que...** — *With all due respect, I think...*
> **Mira, yo lo vería de otra manera.** — *Look, I would see it another way.*

## Useful Discourse Markers

These connectors make your arguments sound structured and fluent:

| Marker | Function | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| además | furthermore / also | Además, hay que considerar... |
| sin embargo | however | Sin embargo, creo que... |
| por otro lado | on the other hand | Por otro lado, también es cierto que... |
| en cambio | on the other hand / instead | En cambio, yo prefiero... |
| de hecho | in fact | De hecho, los estudios muestran... |
| es decir | that is to say | Es decir, necesitamos más tiempo. |
| o sea | I mean / that is (very Mexican) | O sea, no tiene sentido. |
| a fin de cuentas | at the end of the day | A fin de cuentas, lo que importa es... |

## Asking for Opinions

> ¿**Qué opinas**? — *What do you think?*
> ¿**Qué piensas** sobre esto? — *What do you think about this?*
> ¿**Qué te parece**? — *How does it seem to you?*
> ¿**Estás de acuerdo** con eso? — *Do you agree with that?*
> ¿**Cuál es tu opinión** sobre...? — *What is your opinion about...?*

## 🗣️ Cultural Note: Debate Style in Mexico

Mexican debate culture is warm and animated, but with important social rules. Direct confrontation is often softened — Mexicans rarely say "you're wrong" outright. Instead they soften disagreement:

> — Mira, yo lo vería diferente... — *Look, I'd see it differently...*
> — Con todo respeto, creo que... — *With all due respect, I think...*

A uniquely Mexican expression for gentle pushback:

> **"Pues, no sé..."** — *Well, I don't know...* (often means: I disagree but I'm being polite about it)

And the very Mexican **"o sea"** — used constantly as a discourse marker similar to "I mean" or "like" in English:

> — O sea, ¿cómo crees que vamos a terminar esto a tiempo? — *I mean, how do you think we're going to finish this on time?*

Debates about fútbol, food, politics, and telenovelas are national pastimes — and knowing these opinion phrases will make you a full participant in Mexican conversation.
