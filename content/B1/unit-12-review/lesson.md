# B1 Review & Checkpoint

You've covered the full B1 level — intermediate fluency. This unit consolidates everything with a comprehensive review of grammar, vocabulary, and cultural knowledge.

## Grammar Review

### The Subjunctive
Triggered by wishes, doubts, emotions, and impersonal expressions when there are two different subjects:

> Espero que **vengas** conmigo. — *I hope you come with me.*
> Es importante que **hables** con el doctor. — *It's important you speak with the doctor.*
> Ojalá que **haya** suficiente tiempo. — *Hopefully there's enough time.*

Key irregular subjunctive forms: sea, esté, vaya, haya, sepa, dé.

### Future Tense
Add future endings directly to the infinitive (-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -án):

> El año que viene **estudiaré** en México. — *Next year I will study in Mexico.*
> ¿**Podrás** venir mañana? — *Will you be able to come tomorrow?*

Also expresses probability about the present:
> **Será** mi vecino. — *It's probably my neighbor.*

Key irregular stems: tendr-, podr-, har-, dir-, saldr-, vendr-.

### Conditional Tense
Same irregular stems as future, with -ía endings:

> **Quisiera** un café, por favor. — *I would like a coffee, please.*
> ¿**Podrías** ayudarme? — *Could you help me?*
> Si pudiera, **viajaría** por todo el mundo. — *If I could, I would travel the world.*

### Por vs. Para
> **Para** = destination, purpose, deadline, recipient, opinion
> **Por** = cause/reason, duration, exchange, means, movement through

> Compré esto **para** ti **por** cincuenta pesos. — *I bought this for you for fifty pesos.*

### Relative Clauses
> **Que** — for people and things: *El libro que leí...*
> **Quien/quienes** — for people after prepositions: *La persona con quien hablé...*
> **Donde** — for places: *La ciudad donde nací...*
> **Lo que** — what / that which: *Lo que me dijiste me sorprendió.*

### Passive Se & Se Constructions
> Se pasivo: *Se venden tacos.* — Tacos are sold.
> Se impersonal: *Se come bien en Oaxaca.* — The food is good in Oaxaca.
> Se accidental: *Se me olvidó el celular.* — I forgot my phone.

### Idiomatic Verbs (Gustar Pattern)
Subject is the thing; person uses indirect object pronoun:
> Me **encanta** la música. — *I love music.*
> Le **duelen** las rodillas. — *Her knees hurt.*
> ¿Te **falta** algo? — *Are you missing anything?*

## A Full B1-Level Paragraph

> Fíjate que la semana pasada fui a una posada en casa de unos amigos tapatíos. Me dieron la dirección **por** WhatsApp porque no la conocía. Fíjate que el lugar **donde** viven está cañón — tardé una hora en llegar.
>
> Pero valió la pena. Me **encantó** todo: la piñata, el ponche, los tamales... Se **dice** que los tapatíos son los mejores anfitriones de México, y ahora lo entiendo.
>
> Lo que más me **sorprendió** fue que me dieron un bolsito de buñuelos **para** llevar. ¡**Ojalá** que me inviten de nuevo el año que viene!

This paragraph uses: preterite, imperfect, subjunctive (ojalá), por/para, relative clauses (donde), se constructions, idiomatic verbs (encantó, sorprendió), and regional vocabulary (tapatíos, cañón, bolsito).

## B1 Cultural Snapshot

At B1 you now understand:
- How to navigate formal and informal register in spoken and written Spanish
- The major traditions that shape Mexican social life (Día de Muertos, posadas, quinceañera)
- Regional dialects and identity across Mexico
- The subjunctive mood — the gateway to advanced Spanish
- How to express opinions, agree, disagree, and debate in Mexican Spanish

## 🎓 Cultural Note: What B1 Really Means

At B1 (Intermediate), you can now:
- Understand the main points of clear, standard speech on familiar matters
- Handle most travel situations and conversations in Mexico
- Produce simple, connected text on topics of interest
- Describe experiences, events, and briefly explain opinions

The gap between B1 and B2 is often described as the biggest jump in language learning — at B2 you become truly independent. The subjunctive becomes fully mastered, complex if-clauses open up, and nuanced expression becomes natural.

A Mexican saying captures the spirit of continuing the journey:

> **"El que persevera, alcanza."** — *He who perseveres, succeeds.*

¡Ándale, sí se puede!
