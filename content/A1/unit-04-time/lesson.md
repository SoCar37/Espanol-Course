# Days, Months & Time

Knowing how to talk about time is essential for making plans, keeping appointments, and understanding schedules. This unit covers the days of the week, months of the year, and how to tell time in Mexican Spanish.

## Days of the Week

| Spanish | English | Pronunciation |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| lunes | Monday | LOO-nehs |
| martes | Tuesday | MAR-tehs |
| miércoles | Wednesday | MYEHR-koh-lehs |
| jueves | Thursday | HWEH-behs |
| viernes | Friday | BYEHR-nehs |
| sábado | Saturday | SAH-bah-doh |
| domingo | Sunday | doh-MEEN-goh |

> In Spanish, days of the week are **not capitalized** unless they start a sentence.
> The week in Mexico starts on **Monday** (*lunes*), not Sunday.

## Telling Time

To ask the time: **¿Qué hora es?** (What time is it?)

To answer, use **Es la** for 1:00 and **Son las** for all other hours:

> — ¿Qué hora es? *(What time is it?)*
> — Es la una. *(It's one o'clock.)*
> — Son las tres. *(It's three o'clock.)*
> — Son las cinco y media. *(It's five-thirty.)*
> — Son las ocho y cuarto. *(It's eight-fifteen.)*
> — Son las nueve menos cuarto. *(It's eight forty-five / quarter to nine.)*

For a.m. and p.m., add **de la mañana** (morning), **de la tarde** (afternoon), or **de la noche** (evening/night):

> Son las dos de la tarde. *(It's two in the afternoon.)*

## Useful Phrases

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| hoy | today |
| mañana | tomorrow |
| ayer | yesterday |
| esta semana | this week |
| el fin de semana | the weekend |
| ¿A qué hora? | At what time? |

> — ¿A qué hora es la reunión? *(What time is the meeting?)*
> — Es a las tres de la tarde. *(It's at three in the afternoon.)*

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note

In Mexico, time can be flexible in social situations — arriving 15–30 minutes after the stated time is common and socially acceptable for casual gatherings. However, for business meetings and appointments, punctuality is expected. When someone says *ahorita*, it can mean anything from "right now" to "in a little while" to "eventually" — context is everything!
