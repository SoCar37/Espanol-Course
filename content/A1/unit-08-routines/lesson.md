# Daily Routines

Talking about what you do every day is one of the most common conversation topics. This unit covers daily activities, key present-tense verbs, and an introduction to reflexive verbs — the ones used for actions you do to yourself.

## Common Daily Activities

| Spanish | English | Pronunciation |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| despertarse | to wake up | dehs-pehr-TAR-seh |
| levantarse | to get up | leh-bahn-TAR-seh |
| ducharse | to shower | doo-CHAR-seh |
| desayunar | to have breakfast | deh-sah-yoo-NAR |
| trabajar | to work | trah-bah-HAR |
| almorzar | to have lunch | ahl-mor-SAR |
| estudiar | to study | ehs-too-DYAR |
| cenar | to have dinner | seh-NAR |
| acostarse | to go to bed | ah-kohs-TAR-seh |
| dormir | to sleep | dor-MEER |

## Reflexive Verbs

Reflexive verbs describe actions done to yourself. They use reflexive pronouns:

| Pronoun | Reflexive |
|---------|-----------|
| yo | me |
| tú | te |
| él/ella/usted | se |

> **Me** despierto a las siete. *(I wake up at seven.)*
> **Te** duchas por la mañana. *(You shower in the morning.)*
> **Se** levanta tarde los domingos. *(He/She gets up late on Sundays.)*

## Present Tense: Regular -AR Verbs

Most daily routine verbs end in **-ar**. Here's how to conjugate **trabajar** (to work):

| Person | Conjugation |
|--------|------------|
| yo | trabajo |
| tú | trabajas |
| él/ella/usted | trabaja |
| nosotros | trabajamos |
| ustedes | trabajan |

## Sample Dialogue

> — ¿A qué hora te despiertas? *(What time do you wake up?)*
> — Me despierto a las seis de la mañana. *(I wake up at six in the morning.)*
> — ¿Y a qué hora llegas al trabajo? *(And what time do you get to work?)*
> — Llego a las ocho. Primero desayuno, luego me ducho. *(I get there at eight. First I have breakfast, then I shower.)*

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note

In Mexico, the main meal of the day is **la comida** — eaten between 2pm and 4pm, not at noon. Dinner (*la cena*) is lighter and often later, around 8–9pm. Many businesses close during the early afternoon for this extended lunch period. Breakfast (*el desayuno*) is usually substantial — eggs, beans, tortillas, and fresh juice are common.
