# Places & Directions

Getting around is one of the most practical skills in any language. Whether you're navigating a city, asking where the bathroom is, or finding a market, this unit gives you the vocabulary to get there.

## Asking for Directions

> — Perdón, ¿dónde está el banco? *(Excuse me, where is the bank?)*
> — Está a dos cuadras de aquí. *(It's two blocks from here.)*

> — ¿Cómo llego al mercado? *(How do I get to the market?)*
> — Siga derecho y doble a la derecha. *(Go straight and turn right.)*

## Prepositions of Place

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| en | in / at |
| al lado de | next to |
| enfrente de | in front of |
| detrás de | behind |
| entre | between |

> El banco está **enfrente del** parque. *(The bank is in front of the park.)*
> La farmacia está **al lado de** la tienda. *(The pharmacy is next to the store.)*

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note

In Mexican cities, asking locals for directions is very common and people are generally very helpful. Mexicans often give directions using landmarks (*monumentos*, churches, well-known businesses) rather than street names and numbers. In Mexico City, the **metro** is one of the best ways to get around — each station has a unique icon so even non-readers can navigate. *¡El Metro es muy barato!* (The metro is very cheap!)
