# Greetings and Introductions

Welcome to your first lesson in Mexican Spanish! By the end of this unit you will be able to greet people at different times of day, introduce yourself, ask someone's name, and say goodbye — all in natural Mexican Spanish.

---

## Common greetings

In Mexican Spanish, greetings change depending on the time of day. Unlike English, which uses "hello" for almost everything, Spanish has distinct greetings for morning, afternoon, and evening.

| Spanish | English | When to use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Hola | Hello / Hi | Any time — the most casual and versatile greeting |
| Buenos días | Good morning | From sunrise until about noon |
| Buenas tardes | Good afternoon | From noon until sunset (~7pm) |
| Buenas noches | Good evening / Good night | From sunset onward |

> **¡Hola! ¿Cómo estás?**
> Hello! How are you?

A common shortcut in Mexico: people often say just **"buenas"** as a friendly abbreviated greeting at any time of day. You'll hear this constantly.

---

## Asking how someone is

After greeting someone, Mexicans commonly ask how the other person is doing. Here are the most natural phrases:

**¿Cómo estás?** — How are you? *(informal — use with friends, peers, younger people)*

**¿Cómo está usted?** — How are you? *(formal — use with elders, strangers, authority figures)*

**¿Qué tal?** — What's up? / How's it going? *(very casual, very common in Mexico)*

**¿Cómo te va?** — How's it going for you? *(informal, friendly)*

### Common responses:

> **Bien, gracias. ¿Y tú?**
> Fine, thank you. And you?

> **Muy bien, ¿y usted?**
> Very well, and you? *(formal)*

> **Más o menos.**
> So-so. / More or less.

> **Todo bien.**
> All good.

## Introducing yourself

To give your name in Mexican Spanish:

**Me llamo...** — My name is... *(literally: I call myself)*

**Soy...** — I am... *(also used for names, slightly more casual)*

**Mi nombre es...** — My name is... *(slightly more formal)*

> **Hola, me llamo Carlos. ¿Y tú, cómo te llamas?**
> Hello, my name is Carlos. And you, what is your name?

> **Mucho gusto.**
> Nice to meet you. *(literally: much pleasure)*

> **El gusto es mío.**
> The pleasure is mine. *(a polite response to mucho gusto)*

## Saying goodbye

| Spanish | English | Register |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Adiós | Goodbye | Standard |
| Hasta luego | See you later | Very common |
| Hasta mañana | See you tomorrow | When you'll see them tomorrow |
| Hasta pronto | See you soon | When you expect to meet again soon |
| Nos vemos | See you / We'll see each other | Casual, very Mexican |
| Cuídate | Take care | Warm, friendly farewell |
| Chao | Bye | Casual, borrowed from Italian |

---

## 🇲🇽 Cultural note

In Mexico, greetings are an important social ritual — never skip them! Even in brief encounters, Mexicans typically exchange greetings before getting to business. Walking into a shop, entering a room, or starting a conversation without greeting first can come across as rude.

Physical greetings: between friends and family, a single cheek kiss (right cheek) is common among women and between men and women. Men typically shake hands with each other. When in doubt, follow the other person's lead.

**Usted vs. tú:** Mexican Spanish uses *tú* (informal) with friends, family, and peers, and *usted* (formal) with elders, strangers, and people in authority. This course defaults to *tú* but will always flag when *usted* is more appropriate.
