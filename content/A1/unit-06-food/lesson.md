# Food & Drink

Food is at the heart of Mexican culture. Whether you're ordering at a restaurant, shopping at a market, or eating at a friend's home, knowing food vocabulary will serve you every single day.

## Ordering Food

> — ¿Qué va a ordenar? *(What will you order? — formal)*
> — Quiero un taco de pollo, por favor. *(I want a chicken taco, please.)*

> — ¿Qué desea tomar? *(What would you like to drink?)*
> — Me trae un agua, por favor. *(Bring me a water, please.)*

> — La cuenta, por favor. *(The bill, please.)*

## Expressing Likes and Dislikes

Use **me gusta** for singular and **me gustan** for plural:

> Me gusta el café. *(I like coffee.)*
> Me gustan los tacos. *(I like tacos.)*
> No me gusta la sopa. *(I don't like soup.)*

To ask: **¿Te gusta...?** (Do you like...?)

> — ¿Te gusta el chile? *(Do you like chili?)*
> — Sí, me gusta mucho. *(Yes, I like it a lot.)*
> — No, no me gusta. Es muy picante. *(No, I don't like it. It's very spicy.)*

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note

Mexican cuisine is so significant it's recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage. The *taco* is the everyday staple — filled with anything from *carne asada* to *pollo* to *nopales* (cactus). When someone invites you to eat, saying *¡Buen provecho!* (enjoy your meal) as others begin eating is good manners. Asking for the bill (*la cuenta*) is always done explicitly — waiters in Mexico do not bring it automatically.
