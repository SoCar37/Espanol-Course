# Unit 7: Argumentation & Persuasion

Being able to express an opinion is B1. Being able to argue it, defend it, acknowledge opposing views, and persuade others — that's B2. This unit gives you the language tools to participate in debates, write persuasive essays, and hold your position in conversation.

## Presenting Your Position

Start by clearly stating your stance:

| Expression | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| En mi opinión... | En mi opinión, el transporte público debería ser gratuito. |
| Desde mi punto de vista... | Desde mi punto de vista, hay otras prioridades. |
| A mi modo de ver... | A mi modo de ver, el problema es más complejo. |
| Considero que... | Considero que esta medida es necesaria. |
| Estoy convencido/a de que... | Estoy convencida de que podemos mejorar. |
| Es evidente que... | Es evidente que algo tiene que cambiar. |
| No cabe duda de que... | No cabe duda de que el sistema falla. |

> **"No cabe duda de que algo tiene que cambiar."**
> *"There's no doubt that something has to change."* — strong, assertive opener.

## Supporting Your Argument

| Expression | Function |
|-----------|---------|
| Primero que nada... | First of all... |
| Hay que tener en cuenta que... | One must take into account that... |
| Cabe señalar que... | It should be noted that... |
| Los datos muestran que... | The data shows that... |
| Un ejemplo claro es... | A clear example is... |
| Basta con ver que... | You only need to look at... |
| Esto se debe a que... | This is due to the fact that... |
| En otras palabras... | In other words... |

> **"Hay que tener en cuenta que no todos tienen acceso a internet."**
> *"One must take into account that not everyone has internet access."*

## Conceding a Point (Without Losing Your Position)

This is the mark of a sophisticated arguer — acknowledging the other side before redirecting:

| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Si bien es cierto que... | While it is true that... |
| Es verdad que..., pero... | It's true that..., but... |
| Aunque reconozco que... | Although I acknowledge that... |
| No niego que..., sin embargo... | I don't deny that..., however... |
| Tiene razón en que..., no obstante... | You're right that..., nevertheless... |
| Concedo que..., pero hay que considerar... | I concede that..., but one must consider... |

> **"Es verdad que la propuesta tiene méritos, pero los costos son insostenibles."**
> *"It's true that the proposal has merits, but the costs are unsustainable."*

The pattern is always: **acknowledge → redirect with a contrast connector**.

## Countering an Argument

| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Sin embargo, hay que considerar... | However, one must consider... |
| Por el contrario... | On the contrary... |
| Lejos de solucionar el problema... | Far from solving the problem... |
| Eso no toma en cuenta que... | That doesn't take into account that... |
| El argumento falla en que... | The argument fails in that... |
| No es tan simple como parece. | It's not as simple as it seems. |
| Habría que preguntarse si... | One would have to ask whether... |

> **"Por el contrario, los estudios demuestran justo lo opuesto."**
> *"On the contrary, studies demonstrate exactly the opposite."*

## Rhetorical Questions

Rhetorical questions are powerful persuasion tools — they engage the listener and imply an obvious answer:

> **"¿Acaso no es responsabilidad del gobierno proteger a sus ciudadanos?"**
> *"Isn't it the government's responsibility to protect its citizens?"*

> **"¿Cuánto tiempo más vamos a tolerar esta situación?"**
> *"How much longer are we going to tolerate this situation?"*

> **"¿Qué clase de sociedad queremos construir?"**
> *"What kind of society do we want to build?"*

The word *acaso* at the start of a question signals it is rhetorical — the speaker implies the answer is obvious.

## Emphasis and Hedging

### Emphasizing
| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Lo que es más importante... | What is most important... |
| Sobre todo... | Above all... |
| En especial... | Especially... |
| Sin lugar a dudas... | Without a doubt... |
| Definitivamente... | Definitely... |

### Hedging (softening a claim)
| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| En cierta medida... | To a certain extent... |
| Hasta cierto punto... | Up to a point... |
| Podría decirse que... | One could say that... |
| En términos generales... | In general terms... |
| Tiende a ser... | It tends to be... |

> **"Hasta cierto punto, entiendo la postura del gobierno."**
> *"Up to a point, I understand the government's position."*

## Closing an Argument

| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| En conclusión... | In conclusion... |
| Para concluir... | To conclude... |
| En resumen... | In summary... |
| Por todo lo anterior... | For all the above reasons... |
| Queda claro que... | It is clear that... |
| Por eso defiendo que... | That is why I argue that... |

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note: Debate Style in Mexico

Mexican debate culture tends to be warm and indirect compared to northern European or US styles. Outright confrontation — saying bluntly "you're wrong" — is often avoided in favor of *concede-then-redirect* patterns: acknowledging merit in the other person's view before presenting yours. This isn't weakness — it's a social skill deeply rooted in Mexican values of respect and face-saving (*quedar bien*). The most persuasive Mexicans are often those who make you feel heard before they change your mind.
