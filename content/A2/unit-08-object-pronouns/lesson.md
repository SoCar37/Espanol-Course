# Direct & Indirect Object Pronouns

Object pronouns let you avoid repeating nouns and make your Spanish flow naturally. Mastering them is a major step toward sounding fluent.

## Direct Object Pronouns

A **direct object** answers the question *What?* or *Whom?* after the verb.

> Compré **los tacos**. → **Los** compré. — *I bought the tacos. → I bought them.*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | me (me) | nos (us) |
| 2nd | te (you) | os* |
| 3rd masc. | lo (him/it) | los (them) |
| 3rd fem. | la (her/it) | las (them) |

*os is not used in Mexican Spanish — ustedes/los/las covers this.

> ¿Viste la película? — Sí, **la** vi ayer. — *Did you see the movie? — Yes, I saw it yesterday.*

> ¿Me escuchas? — Sí, **te** escucho. — *Do you hear me? — Yes, I hear you.*

## Indirect Object Pronouns

An **indirect object** answers *To whom?* or *For whom?* the action is done.

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | me (to me) | nos (to us) |
| 2nd | te (to you) | — |
| 3rd | le (to him/her/you) | les (to them/you all) |

> Le **mandé** un mensaje a mi mamá. — *I sent my mom a message.* (to her = le)

> El maestro **nos** explicó la gramática. — *The teacher explained grammar to us.*

> ¿Puedes **decirme** la verdad? — *Can you tell me the truth?*

## Placement Rules

Object pronouns go **before** a conjugated verb:

> **Lo** compré en el mercado. — *I bought it at the market.*

Or they can **attach to the end** of an infinitive or -ando form:

> Voy a **comprarlo**. = **Lo** voy a comprar. — *I'm going to buy it.*

> Estoy **escuchándote**. = **Te** estoy escuchando. — *I'm listening to you.*

## Using Both Together: Double Object Pronouns

When you use both a direct and indirect object pronoun together, the **indirect always comes first**:

> **Indirect + Direct + Verb**

> Te lo mando. — *I'll send it to you.*

> Nos la explicó. — *She explained it to us.*

**Important:** When the indirect pronoun is **le** or **les** and is followed by **lo/la/los/las**, change **le/les → se**:

> ~~Le lo~~ → **Se lo** mandé. — *I sent it to him/her.*

> ~~Les las~~ → **Se las** compré. — *I bought them for them.*

This **se** is NOT reflexive — it's just a pronunciation rule to avoid two l-sounds together.

## Le — The All-Purpose Indirect Pronoun

In Mexican Spanish, **le** gets used very flexibly and even appears in expressions where grammatically you might not expect it:

> — ¿Cómo **le** va? — *How's it going (for you)?* (formal)

> — ¡**Échenle** ganas! — *Give it your all!* (literally: throw energy at it)

> — Ya **le** — *Alright / Got it / Let's do it* (very Mexican, very common)

---

## 🛒 Cultural Note: Shopping with Object Pronouns

Mexican markets and shops are where object pronouns shine. Vendors use them constantly:

> — ¿**Lo/La** quiere en bolsa? — *Do you want it in a bag?*

> — **Se lo** envuelvo? — *Shall I wrap it for you?*

> — ¿**Le** doy cambio? — *Shall I give you change?*

Learning to respond naturally — **"Sí, dámelo"** (*Yes, give it to me*) — is the mark of a fluent shopper in any Mexican mercado.
