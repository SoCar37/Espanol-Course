# A2 Review & Checkpoint

You've covered a lot of ground. This unit brings it all together with a comprehensive review of the A2 level — the grammar, the vocabulary, and the cultural fluency you've built.

## What You've Mastered at A2

### Verb Tenses
You now have three tenses in your toolkit:

| Tense | Use | Example |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Present | Ongoing actions and habits now | Trabajo todos los días. |
| Preterite | Completed past events | Ayer fui al mercado. |
| Imperfect | Background, habits, states in the past | Cuando era niño, jugaba afuera. |

**The key distinction:** The preterite moves the story forward; the imperfect sets the stage.

> **Caminaba** por el parque cuando de repente **vi** a mi amigo. — *I was walking in the park when I suddenly saw my friend.*

### Reflexive Verbs
Actions you do to yourself use reflexive pronouns (me, te, se, nos):

> Me **levanto**, me **ducho** y me **visto** cada mañana. — *I get up, shower, and get dressed every morning.*

### Ser vs. Estar
- **Ser** → permanent, defining characteristics (identity, origin, profession, material, time)
- **Estar** → temporary states, location, feelings, ongoing actions

> **Soy** mexicano y **estoy** muy orgulloso de eso. — *I am Mexican and I am very proud of that.*

### Object Pronouns
Direct (lo, la, los, las) and indirect (me, te, le, nos, les) pronouns replace nouns and bring natural flow:

> — ¿Le diste el regalo a tu mamá? — *Did you give your mom the gift?*
> — Sí, **se lo** di ayer. — *Yes, I gave it to her yesterday.*

### The Preterite — Regular and Irregular
You know both regular endings (-é/-aste/-ó for -AR; -í/-iste/-ió for -ER/-IR) and the key irregulars: fui/fue, tuve, hice, dije, vine, puse, supe.

## A2 Vocabulary Themes
You've built vocabulary across six real-world themes:
- **Daily routines** — reflexive verbs, morning and evening habits
- **Descriptions** — ser/estar with adjectives
- **Storytelling** — past tenses with time expressions
- **Expressions** — Mexican idioms and slang (¡Órale!, ¡Qué padre!, buena onda)
- **Shopping** — markets, prices, bargaining, ¿Me hace precio?
- **Travel** — transportation, directions, airport and bus vocabulary

## A Full A2-Level Story

Read this short story using everything you've learned:

> El mes pasado **fui** de vacaciones a Oaxaca. **Era** mi primer viaje solo. Cuando **llegué** al aeropuerto, **estaba** un poco nervioso, pero emocionado.
>
> Tomé un camión del aeropuerto al centro. El conductor **era** muy amable y me **dijo** dónde bajarme. El hotel **quedaba** a dos cuadras de la plaza principal.
>
> Al día siguiente, **fui** al mercado. **Había** un chorro de cosas increíbles. Una señora **me ofreció** una muestra de mole y me **preguntó**: "¿Le **gusta**?" — "¡Está riquísimo!" **le respondí**. **Terminé** comprando tres frascos.
>
> **Fue** un viaje increíble. Oaxaca **es** una ciudad que te **roba** el corazón.

Notice how the story uses preterite for completed events (fui, llegué, dije, compré), imperfect for background states (era, estaba, quedaba, había), and object pronouns naturally (me ofreció, le respondí).

## 🎓 Cultural Note: What It Means to Reach A2

At A2, you can now handle everyday situations in Mexico — introducing yourself, talking about your past, shopping at a market, taking a bus, and understanding the cultural rhythms of Mexican life.

You can follow a conversation at a slower pace, catch most of what vendors and locals say to you, and express yourself on familiar topics. The jump to B1 brings the **subjunctive**, **future tense**, and the ability to discuss opinions, hypotheticals, and complex ideas.

The most important thing at this stage: **use what you know**. Make mistakes. Mexicans are famously warm and patient with Spanish learners — and the effort of trying in Spanish is always appreciated.

> — ¡Ándale, sí se puede! — *Come on, you can do it!*
