# Professional & Academic Writing

Written communication in professional and academic contexts in Mexico has its own conventions, vocabulary, and register. Mastering these allows you to write reports, proposals, academic papers, and formal correspondence that read as native and authoritative.

## Academic Writing Conventions

Mexican academic writing follows conventions derived from both Spanish tradition and international academic norms. Key features:

- **Impersonal constructions** are preferred over first person in formal essays:
  > *Se argumenta que...* — It is argued that...
  > *Se puede observar que...* — It can be observed that...
  > *El presente trabajo tiene como objetivo...* — The present work aims to...

- **Nominalization** (covered in Unit 1) is heavily used to create a formal, abstract tone:
  > *El análisis de los datos revela...* (not: *Cuando analizamos los datos, vemos...*)

- **Citations** follow established formats (APA, Chicago, or the Mexican *Normas APA adaptadas*). Attribution phrases include:
  > *Según [autor] ([año])...* — According to [author] ([year])...
  > *Como señala [autor]...* — As [author] points out...
  > *De acuerdo con [autor]...* — In accordance with [author]...

## Structure of an Academic Essay (*Ensayo Académico*)

| Section | Purpose | Key phrases |
|---|---|---|
| *Introducción* | State topic, context, thesis | *El presente ensayo examina... / Se plantea la hipótesis de que...* |
| *Marco teórico* | Review existing theory/literature | *Desde la perspectiva de... / Autores como X sostienen que...* |
| *Desarrollo* | Present arguments and evidence | *En primer lugar... / Asimismo... / Por otro lado...* |
| *Conclusiones* | Synthesize; restate thesis | *A partir de lo expuesto... / En suma, se puede concluir que...* |
| *Referencias* | Cite all sources used | APA or Chicago format |

## Professional Correspondence

Formal letters and emails in Mexican Spanish follow a specific structure:

**Opening formulas:**
> *Estimado/a [título] [apellido]:* — Dear [title] [surname]: (formal)
> *Por medio de la presente, me dirijo a usted para...* — By means of this letter, I am writing to...
> *Me permito comunicarle que...* — I am writing to inform you that...

**Closing formulas:**
> *En espera de su respuesta, quedo a sus órdenes.* — Awaiting your reply, I remain at your service.
> *Sin otro particular, reciba un cordial saludo.* — With nothing further to add, please receive a cordial greeting.
> *Atentamente,* — Sincerely / Respectfully,

## Writing Reports and Proposals (*Informes y Propuestas*)

A professional report (*informe*) typically includes:

1. *Resumen ejecutivo* — Executive summary (key findings in 1–2 paragraphs)
2. *Antecedentes* — Background and context
3. *Metodología* — Methods used
4. *Resultados* — Findings
5. *Conclusiones y recomendaciones* — Conclusions and recommendations

Key phrases for recommendations:
> *Se recomienda que + subjuntivo...* — It is recommended that...
> *Convendría + infinitivo...* — It would be advisable to...
> *Se propone la implementación de...* — The implementation of... is proposed.

## Abstract Writing (*El Resumen / Abstract*)

A well-written abstract in Spanish covers: objective, methods, key findings, and conclusions — in 150–250 words. It must be written entirely in the third person or with impersonal constructions:

> *El presente estudio analiza... Se empleó una metodología... Los resultados indican que... Se concluye que...*

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note: La UNAM y la Escritura Académica

The Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM) is one of the largest and most prestigious universities in Latin America, and a major arbiter of academic writing standards in Mexican Spanish. Its style guide (*Manual de estilo UNAM*) and the publication norms of its journals set the benchmark for academic prose in Mexico. UNAM also publishes extensively in open access, making Mexican academic writing widely available. If you want to read authentic, high-quality academic Spanish, UNAM's online publications are an outstanding resource.
