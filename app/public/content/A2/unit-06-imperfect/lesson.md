# The Imperfect Tense

The **imperfect** (el imperfecto) is the second major past tense in Spanish. While the preterite describes completed, specific events, the imperfect describes ongoing states, habits, and background conditions in the past.

## Imperfect Endings

The imperfect is one of the most regular tenses in Spanish — only three verbs are irregular.

### -AR Verbs (hablar)

| Pronoun | Ending | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| yo | -aba | hablaba |
| tú | -abas | hablabas |
| usted / él / ella | -aba | hablaba |
| nosotros | -ábamos | hablábamos |
| ustedes / ellos | -aban | hablaban |

### -ER and -IR Verbs (comer / vivir)

| Pronoun | Ending | Example (comer) |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| yo | -ía | comía |
| tú | -ías | comías |
| usted / él / ella | -ía | comía |
| nosotros | -íamos | comíamos |
| ustedes / ellos | -ían | comían |

## The Three Irregular Imperfects

Only three verbs are irregular in the imperfect — worth memorizing immediately:

| Verb | Yo | Tú | Él/ella | Nosotros | Ellos |
|------|----|----|---------|----------|-------|
| ser | era | eras | era | éramos | eran |
| ir | iba | ibas | iba | íbamos | iban |
| ver | veía | veías | veía | veíamos | veían |

> Cuando era niño, **iba** al parque todos los domingos. — *When I was a child, I went to the park every Sunday.*

## When to Use the Imperfect

**1. Habitual or repeated past actions** (used to, would)
> De niño, **comía** tamales en Navidad. — *As a child, I used to eat tamales at Christmas.*

> Mis abuelos **vivían** en Jalisco. — *My grandparents used to live in Jalisco.*

**2. Ongoing background states or descriptions**
> **Hacía** calor y el cielo **estaba** despejado. — *It was hot and the sky was clear.*

> Ella **tenía** el pelo largo. — *She had long hair.*

**3. Age in the past**
> Mi mamá **tenía** treinta años cuando nació mi hermano. — *My mom was thirty years old when my brother was born.*

**4. Time in the past**
> **Eran** las tres de la tarde cuando llegamos. — *It was three in the afternoon when we arrived.*

**5. Two actions happening at the same time**
> Yo **estudiaba** mientras mi hermano **veía** la tele. — *I was studying while my brother was watching TV.*

## Key Time Expressions for the Imperfect

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| cuando era niño/a | when I was a child |
| de pequeño/a | when I was little |
| siempre | always |
| nunca | never |
| todos los días | every day |
| a veces | sometimes |
| generalmente | generally |
| antes | before / in the past |
| en aquel entonces | back then |

> **Siempre** llegábamos tarde a la escuela. — *We always arrived late to school.*

> **Antes** no había tantos celulares. — *Before, there weren't so many cell phones.*

## 🏘️ Cultural Note: Mexican Childhood Nostalgia

Mexicans often speak fondly of childhood with the imperfect tense. A common topic is the neighborhood ice cream vendor — **el paletero** — whose cart you could hear coming from blocks away.

> El paletero siempre **pasaba** por nuestra calle a las cuatro. Nosotros **salíamos** corriendo cuando lo **escuchábamos**. — *The paletero always came down our street at four. We would run out when we heard him.*

This kind of nostalgic storytelling — describing what life *used to be like* — is where the imperfect truly shines in Mexican Spanish.
