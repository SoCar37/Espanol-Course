# Reflexive Verbs & Daily Routines

Reflexive verbs are essential for talking about daily routines — things you do *to yourself*. Once you master them, you can describe your whole day in Spanish.

## What Is a Reflexive Verb?

A reflexive verb is one where the subject and the object are the same person — you perform the action on yourself. In Spanish, reflexive verbs are identified by the pronoun **se** attached to the infinitive.

> **lavar** = to wash (something)
> **lavarse** = to wash oneself

## Reflexive Pronouns

Every subject pronoun has a matching reflexive pronoun:

| Subject | Reflexive | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| yo | me | me lavo |
| tú | te | te lavas |
| usted / él / ella | se | se lava |
| nosotros | nos | nos lavamos |
| ustedes / ellos | se | se lavan |

The reflexive pronoun goes **before** the conjugated verb.

## Common Reflexive Verbs for Daily Routines

| Infinitive | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| despertarse (e→ie) | to wake up |
| levantarse | to get up |
| ducharse | to shower |
| bañarse | to bathe |
| lavarse | to wash (oneself) |
| peinarse | to comb one's hair |
| maquillarse | to put on makeup |
| afeitarse | to shave |
| vestirse (e→i) | to get dressed |
| acostarse (o→ue) | to go to bed |
| dormirse (o→ue) | to fall asleep |

## A Typical Morning Routine

> Me **despierto** a las seis de la mañana. — *I wake up at six in the morning.*

> Me **levanto**, me **ducho** y me **visto** rápido. — *I get up, shower, and get dressed quickly.*

> Mi esposa se **maquilla** mientras yo me **afeito**. — *My wife puts on makeup while I shave.*

> Nos **lavamos** los dientes antes de salir. — *We brush our teeth before leaving.*

## Placement With Infinitives

When a reflexive verb follows another verb (like *querer* or *necesitar*), you have two options — both are correct:

> **Me quiero duchar** = **Quiero ducharme** — *I want to shower.*

> **Te necesitas despertar temprano** = **Necesitas despertarte temprano** — *You need to wake up early.*

The pronoun can either go before the first verb or attach to the end of the infinitive.

## Non-Routine Uses of Reflexive Verbs

Some verbs change meaning when made reflexive:

| Non-reflexive | Reflexive |
|--------------|-----------|
| dormir — to sleep | dormirse — to fall asleep |
| ir — to go | irse — to leave / to go away |
| llamar — to call | llamarse — to be named |
| poner — to put | ponerse — to put on (clothing) / to become |

> Me **llamo** Carlos. — *My name is Carlos.* (Literally: I call myself Carlos.)

> Se **puso** enojado. — *He got / became angry.*

## 🌅 Cultural Note: Morning Rhythms in Mexico

Mexican daily routines often look different from what English speakers expect. In many Mexican households, the main meal of the day (**la comida**) happens between 2–4 PM, not at noon. Dinner (**la cena**) is usually lighter and later — often after 8 PM.

Many Mexicans also use the term **"tantita"** (a little bit) when describing their routine — a very Mexican way of softening requests or descriptions:

> — Espérame tantita, me estoy arreglando. — *Wait for me just a sec, I'm getting ready.*
