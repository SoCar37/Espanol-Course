# Travel & Transportation

Mexico is a vast country with a rich network of transportation options — from the iconic metro in CDMX to long-distance buses connecting every corner of the republic. This unit gets you moving.

## At the Airport

> ¿Dónde está la sala de espera? — *Where is the waiting room?*

> ¿A qué hora sale el vuelo? — *What time does the flight leave?*

> ¿Puedo llevar esta maleta de mano? — *Can I take this bag as carry-on?*

> Mi vuelo sale a las ocho y media. — *My flight leaves at eight thirty.*

> Tengo que facturar el equipaje. — *I have to check my luggage.*

## Taking the Bus

Long-distance buses in Mexico are excellent — many have reclining seats, movies, and air conditioning.

> ¿De dónde salen los autobuses a Oaxaca? — *Where do buses to Oaxaca leave from?*

> ¿A qué hora sale el próximo camión? — *What time does the next bus leave?*

> Quiero un boleto de ida a Guadalajara. — *I'd like a one-way ticket to Guadalajara.*

> Un boleto de ida y vuelta, por favor. — *A round-trip ticket, please.*

> ¿Cuánto tarda el viaje? — *How long does the trip take?*

## Getting a Taxi or Uber

> ¿Me puede llevar a...? — *Can you take me to...?*

> ¿Cuánto me cobra hasta el centro? — *How much do you charge to downtown?*

> ¿Pone el taxímetro? — *Do you use the meter?* (always ask in Mexico!)

> Voy a pedir un Uber. — *I'm going to order an Uber.*

> ¿Cuánto está el viaje? — *How much is the trip?*

## Asking for Directions

> Perdón, ¿cómo llego a...? — *Excuse me, how do I get to...?*

> ¿Queda cerca / lejos de aquí? — *Is it near / far from here?*

> Siga derecho. — *Go straight.*

> Doble a la derecha / izquierda. — *Turn right / left.*

> Está a dos cuadras. — *It's two blocks away.*

> Está a la vuelta. — *It's just around the corner.*

## Useful Travel Phrases

> ¿Dónde puedo tomar el metro? — *Where can I take the subway?*

> ¿Esta línea va a...? — *Does this line go to...?*

> Me bajé en la parada equivocada. — *I got off at the wrong stop.*

> Perdí mi boleto. — *I lost my ticket.*

## 🚌 Cultural Note: The Mexican Camión

In Mexico, the word **camión** (literally: truck) refers to city buses — not the big intercity coaches, which are called **autobús** or by brand names like **ADO** or **ETN**. City camiones are famously informal — they often blast music, the driver may have a personal assistant who calls out destinations, and routes can be confusing to newcomers.

When boarding a city camión, you tell the helper where you're going:
> — Al centro. — *Downtown.*
> — Son cuatro pesos. — *That's four pesos.*

The **metro** in Mexico City (Sistema de Transporte Colectivo) is one of the busiest in the world, identified by color-coded lines and iconic logos for each station — designed so that even non-readers can navigate it.
