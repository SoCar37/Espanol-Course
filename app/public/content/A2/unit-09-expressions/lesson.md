# Common Expressions & Idioms

Grammar gets you understood. Expressions make you sound like you actually live in Mexico. This unit covers the phrases that Mexicans use every day — the ones no textbook teaches but everyone uses.

## Everyday Mexican Expressions

### Reactions and Filler Words

> **¡Órale!** — *Alright! / Okay! / Wow! / Let's go!* (the most versatile Mexican word)

> **¡Híjole!** — *Wow! / Oh no! / Yikes!* (surprise or mild shock)

> **¡Qué padre!** — *How cool! / Awesome!* (padre literally means father, but idiomatically = cool)

> **¡Qué onda!** — *What's up! / What's going on!* (casual greeting)

> **No manches** — *No way! / Come on!* (mild exclamation — the polite version of a stronger phrase)

> **Está de pelos** — *It's great / awesome* (literally: it's of hairs)

### Agreement and Confirmation

> **Simón** — *Yes / Exactly* (Mexican slang for sí)

> **Nel** — *No / Nope* (Mexican slang for no)

> **Sale** — *Okay / Deal / Agreed* (from "sale y vale" — shortened)

> **Ándale** — *Okay! / Go ahead! / Exactly!* (agreement or encouragement)

### Talking About People

> **¿Qué pasó, güey?** — *What's up, dude?* (güey/wey is extremely common — used between friends)

> **Es muy buena onda** — *He/She's really cool / chill* (buena onda = good vibes)

> **Es codo** — *He/She's cheap / stingy* (codo = elbow — don't ask why)

> **Me cae bien** — *I like him/her* (literally: he/she falls well on me)

> **Me cae gordo/gorda** — *I can't stand him/her* (literally: he/she falls heavy on me)

### Useful Daily Phrases

> **Ahorita regreso** — *I'll be right back* (ahorita = somewhere between now and later)

> **¿Cómo ves?** — *What do you think? / How does it look to you?*

> **Ya estuvo** — *That's it / All done / It's over* (used to wrap things up)

> **Me late** — *I like it / It feels right to me* (literally: it beats/pulses to me)

> **No hay de qué** — *You're welcome / Don't mention it*

## Expressing Quantity — Very Mexican Style

Mexicans use diminutives and augmentatives constantly to add feeling:

> **tantito/tantita** — a tiny bit (*Espérame tantito* = Wait just a sec)

> **ahorita** — right now / in a moment (the famously ambiguous one)

> **un chorro** — a lot / tons (*Tengo un chorro de trabajo* = I have tons of work)

> **un chingo** — a whole lot (more emphatic, slightly crude but very common)

## Body-Based Idioms

Spanish loves the body for idiomatic expressions:

| Expression | Literal | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| no tener pelos en la lengua | no hairs on the tongue | to be blunt / speak one's mind |
| meter la pata | to put your paw in it | to put your foot in your mouth |
| costar un ojo de la cara | to cost an eye of the face | to cost an arm and a leg |
| dar lata | to give tin/can | to be annoying / to bother |

> Ese carro me **costó un ojo de la cara**. — *That car cost me an arm and a leg.*

> Deja de **dar lata**, ¿sí? — *Stop being annoying, okay?*

## 🎭 Cultural Note: Mexicans and Indirect Communication

Mexican communication style tends to be warm but indirect — saying "no" directly is often considered rude. Instead, you'll hear softened refusals:

> — ¿Puedes venir mañana? — *Can you come tomorrow?*
> — **Ahí viendo...** — *We'll see... / Maybe...* (almost certainly means no)

> — ¿Te gustó la comida? — *Did you like the food?*
> — **Estuvo bien...** — *It was fine...* (could mean anything from okay to secretly not great)

Learning to read between the lines — and to respond diplomatically yourself — is a key part of cultural fluency in Mexico.
