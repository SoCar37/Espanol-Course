# Shopping & Money

Whether you're at a street market, a tianguis, or a shopping mall, knowing how to shop in Spanish opens up Mexico in a completely different way. This unit covers everything from asking prices to bargaining politely.

## Asking About Price and Availability

> ¿Cuánto cuesta? — *How much does it cost?*

> ¿Cuánto cuesta esto / eso? — *How much does this / that cost?*

> ¿Cuánto cuestan? — *How much do they cost?* (plural)

> ¿Tiene en otra talla? — *Do you have it in another size?*

> ¿Tiene en otro color? — *Do you have it in another color?*

> ¿Me lo puedo probar? — *Can I try it on?*

## Making Purchases

> Quiero llevar esto. — *I'd like to take this / I'll take this.*

> ¿Me lo envuelve? — *Could you wrap it for me?*

> ¿Acepta tarjeta? — *Do you accept card?*

> ¿Tiene cambio de...? — *Do you have change for...?*

> ¿Me da un ticket / una nota? — *Can I get a receipt?*

## Bargaining Politely

At markets and tianguis, bargaining is expected — it's part of the culture. The key is to be friendly and smile.

> — ¿Cuánto cuesta esta bolsa? — *How much is this bag?*
> — Trescientos pesos. — *Three hundred pesos.*
> — ¿Me hace precio? ¿Me la deja en doscientos? — *Can you give me a deal? Can you let it go for two hundred?*
> — Le dejo en doscientos cincuenta, ¿sí? — *I'll let it go for two-fifty, okay?*
> — ¡Sale! — *Deal!*

## Sizes and Quantities

> ¿En qué le puedo ayudar? — *How can I help you?*

> Busco algo en talla mediana. — *I'm looking for something in a medium size.*

> ¿Lo tiene en chico / mediano / grande? — *Do you have it in small / medium / large?*

> Me llevo dos. — *I'll take two.*

> ¿Me da un kilo de...? — *Can I have a kilo of...?* (at food markets)

## 🛍️ Cultural Note: The Mexican Tianguis

The **tianguis** is a cornerstone of Mexican commercial culture — an open-air street market that appears on specific days of the week in neighborhoods across the country. Unlike fixed stores, tianguis vendors set up and pack up each week.

At a tianguis you can find everything from fresh produce and homemade food to clothing, tools, and electronics. Prices are negotiable, and the atmosphere is social — vendors call out to passersby:

> — ¡Pásele, pásele! ¿Qué va a llevar? — *Come on in! What are you going to take?*

> — ¡Lleve, lleve! Todo a diez pesitos. — *Take some, take some! Everything for just ten pesos.*

The diminutive **pesitos** (little pesos) is a classic vendor trick to make prices sound smaller and more approachable.
