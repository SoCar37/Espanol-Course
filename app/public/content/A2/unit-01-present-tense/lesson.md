# Present Tense Review & Stem-Changing Verbs

You already know how to conjugate regular verbs in the present tense. Now it's time to level up with **stem-changing verbs** — one of the most common patterns in Spanish.

## Quick Review: Regular Present Tense

Regular verbs follow predictable endings. Here's a quick reminder:

| Pronoun | -AR (hablar) | -ER (comer) | -IR (vivir) |
|---------|-------------|------------|------------|
| yo | hablo | como | vivo |
| tú | hablas | comes | vives |
| usted / él / ella | habla | come | vive |
| nosotros | hablamos | comemos | vivimos |
| ustedes / ellos | hablan | comen | viven |

## What Are Stem-Changing Verbs?

In stem-changing verbs, the vowel in the verb's stem changes in all forms **except** nosotros. This is sometimes called the "boot pattern" because if you circle the changed forms in a conjugation table, they form the shape of a boot.

There are three main patterns:

### E → IE
> **Querer** (to want): quiero, quieres, quiere, queremos, quieren

> **Pensar** (to think): pienso, piensas, piensa, pensamos, piensan

> **Preferir** (to prefer): prefiero, prefieres, prefiere, preferimos, prefieren

### O → UE
> **Poder** (to be able to / can): puedo, puedes, puede, podemos, pueden

> **Volver** (to return): vuelvo, vuelves, vuelve, volvemos, vuelven

> **Dormir** (to sleep): duermo, duermes, duerme, dormimos, duermen

### E → I (only -IR verbs)
> **Pedir** (to ask for / to order): pido, pides, pide, pedimos, piden

> **Servir** (to serve): sirvo, sirves, sirve, servimos, sirven

## Stem-Changers in Action

Here's how these verbs sound in everyday Mexican Spanish:

> — ¿Qué **quieres** comer? — *What do you want to eat?*

> — **Prefiero** tacos, pero **puedo** comer lo que sea. — *I prefer tacos, but I can eat anything.*

> — ¿A qué hora **vuelves** a casa? — *What time do you return home?*

> — **Pido** el mismo plato siempre. — *I always order the same dish.*

## Common Irregular Yo Forms

Some verbs are only irregular in the **yo** form. These are worth memorizing individually:

| Verb | Yo form | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| hacer | hago | to do / to make |
| poner | pongo | to put |
| salir | salgo | to leave / to go out |
| traer | traigo | to bring |
| conocer | conozco | to know (a person/place) |
| saber | sé | to know (a fact) |

> — **Salgo** del trabajo a las seis. — *I leave work at six.*

> — **Hago** ejercicio todos los días. — *I exercise every day.*

## 🌮 Cultural Note: Mexican Verb Habits

In Mexico, you'll hear **ahorita** constantly — it's a uniquely Mexican expression that can mean "right now," "in a little while," or even "sometime later," depending on tone. Context is everything!

> — ¿Puedes ayudarme? — *Can you help me?*
> — Sí, **ahorita** te ayudo. — *Yes, I'll help you right now (…or soon).*

Mexicans also frequently use **andar** (literally "to walk") as an informal substitute for estar when describing ongoing states:

> — ¿Cómo andas? — *How are you doing?* (very casual, very Mexican)
