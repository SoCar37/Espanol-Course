# Preterite Tense — Irregular Verbs

You know how to conjugate regular verbs in the preterite. Now it's time to tackle the irregulars — and the good news is that the most common ones share patterns that make them easier to learn together.

## Completely Irregular: Ser and Ir

**Ser** (to be) and **ir** (to go) share identical preterite forms. Context tells you which one is meant.

| Pronoun | Ser / Ir |
|---------|----------|
| yo | fui |
| tú | fuiste |
| usted / él / ella | fue |
| nosotros | fuimos |
| ustedes / ellos | fueron |

> Ayer **fui** al mercado. — *Yesterday I went to the market.* (ir)

> Ella **fue** muy amable con todos. — *She was very kind to everyone.* (ser)

> **Fuimos** a la playa el fin de semana. — *We went to the beach on the weekend.* (ir)

## The "U-stem" Group

Several common verbs share irregular stems ending in **-u** and use the same set of endings:

| Verb | Irregular stem | Yo form |
|------|---------------|---------|
| tener (to have) | tuv- | tuve |
| estar (to be) | estuv- | estuve |
| poder (to be able to) | pud- | pude |
| poner (to put) | pus- | puse |
| saber (to know) | sup- | supe |

### U-stem endings (no accent marks!)
| Pronoun | Ending |
|---------|--------|
| yo | -e |
| tú | -iste |
| usted / él / ella | -o |
| nosotros | -imos |
| ustedes / ellos | -ieron |

> **Tuve** que trabajar hasta tarde. — *I had to work until late.*

> ¿Dónde **estuviste** anoche? — *Where were you last night?*

> No **pude** terminar a tiempo. — *I couldn't finish on time.*

> **Supimos** la noticia esta mañana. — *We found out the news this morning.*

## The "I-stem" Group

| Verb | Irregular stem | Yo form |
|------|---------------|---------|
| hacer (to do/make) | hic- | hice |
| querer (to want) | quis- | quise |
| venir (to come) | vin- | vine |
| decir (to say) | dij- | dije |
| traer (to bring) | traj- | traje |

> **Hice** la tarea antes de salir. — *I did my homework before leaving.*

> No **quise** ir a la fiesta. — *I didn't want to go to the party.*

> **Vine** tan pronto como pude. — *I came as soon as I could.*

> ¿Qué **dijeron** ellos? — *What did they say?*

> Note: **hacer** → hice (c → z before o: él **hizo**, not hicó, to preserve the /s/ sound)

## Dar and Ver — Short Irregulars

**Dar** (to give) and **ver** (to see) are short but irregular:

| Pronoun | Dar | Ver |
|---------|-----|-----|
| yo | di | vi |
| tú | diste | viste |
| él/ella | dio | vio |
| nosotros | dimos | vimos |
| ellos | dieron | vieron |

> Le **di** un regalo a mi mamá. — *I gave my mom a gift.*

> **Vi** una película increíble anoche. — *I saw an incredible movie last night.*

## 🎬 Cultural Note: Mexican Telenovelas and the Preterite

Irregular preterite verbs come alive in Mexican storytelling. Telenovelas — wildly popular melodramatic TV series — are packed with dramatic past-tense declarations:

> — ¡**Fuiste** tú quien lo hizo! — *It was YOU who did it!*

> — ¡No **pude** evitarlo! — *I couldn't avoid it!*

> — ¿Qué me **dijiste**? — *What did you say to me?*

Even if you never watch a telenovela, you'll hear these constructions constantly in everyday Mexican conversation when people recount what happened. Mastering irregular preterites makes you sound dramatically more fluent.
