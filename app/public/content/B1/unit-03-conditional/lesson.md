# The Conditional Tense

The conditional is "would" in English — used for hypothetical situations, polite requests, and imagined scenarios. The good news: it uses the same irregular stems as the future, with different endings.

## Forming the Conditional

Like the future, the conditional adds endings directly to the **infinitive** (or the same irregular stems). The endings are the same for all verbs:

### Conditional Endings

| Pronoun | Ending |
|---------|--------|
| yo | -ía |
| tú | -ías |
| usted / él / ella | -ía |
| nosotros | -íamos |
| ustedes / ellos | -ían |

### Regular Examples

| Infinitive | Yo | Tú | Él/ella | Nosotros | Ellos |
|-----------|----|----|---------|----------|-------|
| hablar | hablaría | hablarías | hablaría | hablaríamos | hablarían |
| comer | comería | comerías | comería | comeríamos | comerían |
| vivir | viviría | vivirías | viviría | viviríamos | vivirían |

## Irregular Conditional Stems

Same stems as the future — if you know the future irregulars, you know the conditional irregulars:

| Verb | Stem | Yo conditional |
|------|------|---------------|
| tener | tendr- | tendría |
| venir | vendr- | vendría |
| poder | podr- | podría |
| saber | sabr- | sabría |
| hacer | har- | haría |
| decir | dir- | diría |
| salir | saldr- | saldría |
| poner | pondr- | pondría |
| querer | querr- | querría |
| haber | habr- | habría |

## The Three Main Uses

### 1. Hypothetical situations (what would happen)
> ¿Qué **harías** con un millón de dólares? — *What would you do with a million dollars?*
> Yo **viajaría** por todo el mundo. — *I would travel the whole world.*
> **Viviría** en la playa. — *I would live on the beach.*

### 2. Polite requests (softer than the present)
> ¿**Podrías** ayudarme? — *Could you help me?* (more polite than ¿Puedes?)
> ¿**Sería** posible cambiar la fecha? — *Would it be possible to change the date?*
> **Quisiera** un café, por favor. — *I would like a coffee, please.* (very polite)

### 3. If-clauses: Si + imperfect subjunctive... conditional
> **Si tuviera** más tiempo, **estudiaría** más. — *If I had more time, I would study more.*
> **Si pudiera**, **viviría** en México. — *If I could, I would live in Mexico.*

> Note: The imperfect subjunctive (tuviera, pudiera) will be covered at B2. For now, recognize the pattern.

## Conditional vs. Future — Quick Comparison

| Future | Conditional |
|--------|-------------|
| What will happen | What would happen |
| Mañana **iré**. *I will go tomorrow.* | Si pudiera, **iría**. *If I could, I would go.* |
| **Tendrás** tiempo. *You will have time.* | **Tendrías** tiempo si no trabajaras. *You would have time if you didn't work.* |

## Quisiera — The Polite Essential

**Quisiera** (I would like) is the conditional of **querer** and is the gold standard for polite requests in Spanish — more refined than *quiero* in formal or service situations:

> **Quisiera** reservar una mesa para dos. — *I would like to reserve a table for two.*
> **Quisiera** saber más información. — *I would like to know more information.*

## 💬 Cultural Note: Politeness in Mexican Spanish

Mexicans place great value on courtesy and indirect communication. The conditional is a key tool for polite speech. Compare:

> Dame un café. — *Give me a coffee.* (blunt, borderline rude)
> ¿Me da un café? — *Would you give me a coffee?* (neutral)
> ¿Me **podría** dar un café? — *Could you give me a coffee?* (polite)
> **Quisiera** un café, por favor. — *I would like a coffee, please.* (very polite, smooth)

In Mexican service culture, vendors and staff commonly use the conditional to soften offers:
> ¿**Gustaría** algo más? — *Would you like anything else?*
> ¿**Desearía** ver el menú? — *Would you like to see the menu?*
