# Unit 10: Business Spanish

Mexican professional environments have their own vocabulary, formality conventions, and cultural norms. Whether you're attending a meeting, writing a business email, negotiating a contract, or working alongside Mexican colleagues, this unit gives you the language to operate effectively and respectfully.

### Business operations

> **La junta** is the most common Mexican workplace word for a meeting — more so than *reunión* in many contexts. *"Tengo junta a las diez"* = I have a meeting at ten.

## Meetings

### Opening
| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Vamos a dar inicio a la junta. | Let's start the meeting. |
| El orden del día es el siguiente: | The agenda is as follows: |
| El objetivo de esta reunión es... | The objective of this meeting is... |
| Les doy la bienvenida a todos. | I welcome everyone. |

### During
| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Quisiera agregar algo. | I'd like to add something. |
| Con todo respeto, no estoy de acuerdo. | With all due respect, I disagree. |
| ¿Me podría ampliar ese punto? | Could you expand on that point? |
| Quedamos en que... | We agreed that... |

### Closing
| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Quedamos pendientes de... | We are awaiting... |
| El plazo es el viernes. | The deadline is Friday. |
| ¿Queda alguna duda? | Are there any remaining questions? |
| Levantamos la sesión. | We adjourn the meeting. |

## Business Email Conventions

**Formal openings:**
- *Estimado/a [nombre]:* — Dear [name]:
- *Me dirijo a usted con el fin de...* — I am writing to you in order to...
- *Por medio de la presente...* — By means of this correspondence...

**Formal closings:**
- *Quedo a sus órdenes.* — I remain at your service. *(most distinctively Mexican)*
- *En espera de su respuesta...* — Awaiting your response...
- *Saludos cordiales,* — Kind regards,
- *Atentamente,* — Sincerely / Respectfully,

> **"Quedo a sus órdenes"** is the most distinctively Mexican professional email closing — seen constantly, signaling availability and service orientation.

## Negotiations

| Expression | Translation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Nos interesa llegar a un acuerdo. | We are interested in reaching an agreement. |
| ¿Hay posibilidad de negociar el precio? | Is there room to negotiate the price? |
| Eso no entra en nuestro presupuesto. | That doesn't fit within our budget. |
| Estamos dispuestos a ceder en... | We are willing to give ground on... |
| Llegamos a un acuerdo. | We reached an agreement. |

## Professional Titles in Mexico

In Mexico, professional titles are used extensively in formal settings:

| Title | Used for |
|-------|---------|
| licenciado/a (Lic.) | university graduates (very broadly applied) |
| ingeniero/a (Ing.) | engineers |
| doctor/a (Dr./Dra.) | medical doctors and PhDs |
| maestro/a | teachers and holders of master's degrees |
| contador/a | accountants |

> *"Buenos días, licenciado García"* — addressing a colleague formally. Using someone's title shows respect and cultural awareness.

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note: Relationships Before Business

In Mexican business culture, *las relaciones* (relationships) come before transactions. It is expected — and often essential — to spend time building personal rapport before getting down to business. Jumping straight to the agenda without small talk can be seen as cold or disrespectful. Coffee, lunch, and casual conversation are not wasted time — they are how trust is built. Additionally, hierarchy is strongly respected: address superiors as *usted*, use their professional title, and defer to seniority in meetings.
