# Unit 12: B2 Review & Checkpoint

You've covered twelve units of advanced Spanish. This review consolidates the key grammar, vocabulary, and cultural knowledge from B2 — and prepares you for the checkpoint quiz.

## Grammar Review

### Past Subjunctive — Formation

**Ellos preterite → drop -ron → add endings (-ra, -ras, -ra, -ramos, -ran)**

| Verb | Ellos preterite | Yo form |
|------|----------------|---------|
| hablar | hablaron | hablara |
| tener | tuvieron | tuviera |
| ser/ir | fueron | fuera |
| poder | pudieron | pudiera |
| decir | dijeron | dijera |
| saber | supieron | supiera |

**When to use it:**
- Past main clause → subordinate: *Quería que vinieras.*
- Type 2 si clauses: *Si tuviera dinero, viajaría.*
- *Como si* (always): *Habla como si supiera todo.*
- Polite requests: *Quisiera..., pudiera...*
- *Ojalá* (contrary to fact): *Ojalá pudiera ir.*

### Si Clauses — Three Types

| Type | Si clause | Result clause | Reality |
|------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| 1 — Real | si + present indicative | future / present / command | genuinely possible |
| 2 — Hypothetical | si + past subjunctive | conditional | contrary to present reality |
| 3 — Past impossible | si + past perfect subjunctive | conditional perfect | didn't happen |

**Critical rule:** Never use the conditional after *si*.
- ❌ *Si tendría tiempo...* → ✅ *Si tuviera tiempo...*

### Conjunctions and Subjunctive

**Always subjunctive:** *para que, a menos que, sin que, antes de que, con tal de que*

**Subjunctive = future/uncertain; indicative = habitual past:**
*cuando, aunque, hasta que, después de que, en cuanto*

### Connectors by Function

| Function | Key connectors |
|---------|----------------|
| Contrast | sin embargo, no obstante, a pesar de que, sino, en cambio |
| Cause | ya que, como (sentence-initial), debido a |
| Consequence | por lo tanto, así que, en consecuencia |
| Concession | si bien, aunque, con todo |

**Sino vs. pero:** *sino* corrects after a negation; *pero* adds a contrasting comment.

## Cultural Literacy Checklist

By the end of B2 you should know:

- ☑ Mexican dialect markers: *tú/usted/ustedes* (never *vosotros*); *computadora, carro, celular*
- ☑ Octavio Paz — *El laberinto de la soledad*, Nobel Prize, the concept of *la máscara*
- ☑ Juan Rulfo — *Pedro Páramo*, influenced García Márquez
- ☑ Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz — 200-peso note, first feminist voice in the Americas
- ☑ Elena Poniatowska — testimonial literature, *La noche de Tlatelolco*
- ☑ Mexican business: *la junta*, *quedo a sus órdenes*, *licenciado/a*, relationships first
- ☑ Mexican media: *meme* = MEH-meh, *maratonear*, *telenovela*, *mariachi/banda/norteño*
- ☑ Social issues: migration as triple reality, *la desigualdad*, *rendir cuentas*
- ☑ Diminutives: *-ito/a*, *ahorita* ambiguity, *quedar bien*
- ☑ Register: formal ↔ colloquial switching, *quisiera/pudiera* for politeness

## B2 Self-Assessment

At B2 you should be able to:
- Understand the main ideas of complex texts on both concrete and abstract topics
- Interact with a degree of fluency and spontaneity that makes conversation with native speakers possible
- Produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects
- Explain a viewpoint on a topical issue giving advantages and disadvantages of various options
- Use the past subjunctive naturally in conversation and writing
- Shift registers appropriately between formal and colloquial contexts
- Discuss Mexican cultural topics with genuine literacy

If you can do all of the above — congratulations. You're ready for C1.
