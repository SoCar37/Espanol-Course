# Unit 4: Complex Sentences & Connectors

## Connecting Ideas — The Architecture of Fluent Spanish

At B2 level, it is not enough to produce correct sentences. What separates intermediate from advanced speakers is the ability to *connect* ideas smoothly — to add, contrast, cause, elaborate, and conclude in ways that feel natural and cohesive. This unit equips you with the connectors that Spanish speakers use to build extended discourse.

---

## Category 1 — Addition

Use these to add information, reinforce a point, or list items:

| Connector | Meaning | Register |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| además | furthermore / in addition | neutral |
| también | also / too | neutral |
| incluso | even / including | neutral |
| es más | what is more / moreover | formal |
| asimismo | likewise / also | formal |
| de igual manera | in the same way | formal |
| aparte de eso | apart from that | spoken |

> *El candidato tiene experiencia. **Además**, habla tres idiomas.*
> The candidate has experience. Furthermore, he speaks three languages.

> *No solo llegó tarde — **es más**, ni se disculpó.*
> Not only did he arrive late — what is more, he didn't even apologize.

---

## Category 2 — Contrast and Concession

These connectors introduce an opposing or surprising idea:

| Connector | Meaning | Register |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| sin embargo | however / nevertheless | neutral/formal |
| no obstante | nonetheless / however | formal |
| a pesar de (que) | despite / in spite of | neutral |
| aunque | although / even though | neutral |
| si bien | while / although (formal) | formal |
| de todas formas | anyway / regardless | spoken |
| por otro lado | on the other hand | neutral |
| en cambio | on the other hand / instead | neutral |

> *La película recibió críticas negativas. **Sin embargo**, fue un éxito en taquilla.*
> The film received negative reviews. However, it was a box office success.

> ***A pesar de** las dificultades, lograron terminar el proyecto a tiempo.*
> Despite the difficulties, they managed to finish the project on time.

> *El precio es alto. **En cambio**, la calidad es excelente.*
> The price is high. On the other hand, the quality is excellent.

---

## Category 3 — Cause and Effect

These connectors explain reasons or introduce consequences:

| Connector | Meaning | Register |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| porque | because | neutral |
| ya que | since / given that | neutral |
| dado que | given that | formal |
| puesto que | since / seeing that | formal |
| por eso | that is why / therefore | neutral |
| por lo tanto | therefore / thus | formal |
| en consecuencia | as a consequence | formal |
| así que | so / therefore | spoken |
| de ahí que (+ subj.) | hence / that is why | formal |

> ***Ya que** no hay tiempo, lo dejamos para mañana.*
> Since there's no time, we'll leave it for tomorrow.

> *No estudió. **Por lo tanto**, no pasó el examen.*
> He didn't study. Therefore, he didn't pass the exam.

> *Hubo un accidente en la carretera, **así que** llegamos tarde.*
> There was an accident on the road, so we arrived late.

Note that **de ahí que** requires the subjunctive in the following clause:

> *Las redes sociales son adictivas, **de ahí que** mucha gente **pase** horas en el celular.*
> Social media is addictive, hence many people spend hours on their phone.

## Category 4 — Elaboration and Exemplification

| Connector | Meaning | Register |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| es decir | that is to say / in other words | neutral |
| o sea | I mean / in other words | spoken (very common in Mexico) |
| o sea que | so, what that means is | spoken (Mexico) |
| por ejemplo | for example | neutral |
| como | such as / like | neutral |
| en particular | in particular | neutral |
| de hecho | in fact / as a matter of fact | neutral |

> *El proyecto está retrasado, **es decir**, no lo vamos a entregar esta semana.*
> The project is delayed — in other words, we're not going to deliver it this week.

> ***De hecho**, ya lo intenté tres veces.*
> In fact, I already tried it three times.

## Category 5 — Order and Structure

Useful for organizing a longer explanation or argument:

| Connector | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| en primer lugar | in the first place / firstly |
| en segundo lugar | secondly |
| por último / finalmente | finally / lastly |
| por un lado... por otro | on one hand... on the other |
| en cuanto a | as for / regarding |
| con respecto a | with respect to |
| en resumen | in summary |
| en conclusión | in conclusion |

> ***En primer lugar**, hay que analizar el problema. **En segundo lugar**, buscar soluciones. **Por último**, implementarlas.*

## Register — Written vs. Spoken

Many connectors have spoken and formal equivalents. Recognizing both is key to reading comprehension and writing quality:

| Spoken / Informal | Formal / Written |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| o sea | es decir |
| así que | por lo tanto |
| pero | sin embargo / no obstante |
| además | asimismo / es más |
| aunque | si bien |

---

## 🇲🇽 Cultural Note — "O sea" in Mexican Spanish

If you spend any time with Mexican speakers, you will hear **o sea** constantly. It is one of the most productive discourse markers in Mexican Spanish, functioning as *I mean*, *that is*, *so*, and sometimes as a discourse filler similar to English *like* or *you know*:

> *O sea, no es que no quiera ir — o sea, es que tengo mucho trabajo.*
> I mean, it's not that I don't want to go — it's just that I have a lot of work.

While fluent and natural in speech, **o sea** should be avoided in formal or written Spanish. Its formal equivalent is **es decir**. Being able to switch between the two registers is a mark of B2 proficiency.
