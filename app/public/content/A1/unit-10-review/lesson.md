# A1 Review & Checkpoint

Congratulations on reaching the final unit of A1! This unit reviews the key vocabulary and grammar from all nine previous units, consolidates what you've learned, and prepares you for the A1 checkpoint quiz.

## What You've Learned in A1

### Unit 1 — Greetings
You can greet people at any time of day, introduce yourself, and say goodbye using Mexican Spanish expressions like *nos vemos* and *cuídate*.

> **Hola, me llamo Ana. Mucho gusto.** *(Hello, my name is Ana. Nice to meet you.)*

### Unit 2 — Numbers
You can count to 100, give your age, ask for prices, and say phone numbers in pairs.

> **Tengo veinticinco años. Cuesta cincuenta pesos.** *(I'm twenty-five. It costs fifty pesos.)*

### Unit 3 — Colors & Descriptions
You can describe things using colors and adjectives, with correct gender agreement.

> **Mi carro es gris y pequeño.** *(My car is gray and small.)*

### Unit 4 — Days, Months & Time
You can tell the time, name the days and months, and use key time expressions including the uniquely Mexican *ahorita*.

> **Son las tres y media de la tarde.** *(It's three-thirty in the afternoon.)*

### Unit 5 — Family
You can name family members, use possessives, and describe people's characteristics.

> **Mi hermana es alta y simpática.** *(My sister is tall and nice.)*

### Unit 6 — Food & Drink
You can order food and drinks, express likes and dislikes, and navigate a Mexican restaurant.

> **Me gustan los tacos. ¿Me trae la cuenta, por favor?** *(I like tacos. Can you bring me the bill, please?)*

### Unit 7 — Places & Directions
You can ask where places are, give and follow directions, and use prepositions of place.

> **¿Dónde está la farmacia? Está a dos cuadras, a la derecha.** *(Where's the pharmacy? It's two blocks away, on the right.)*

### Unit 8 — Daily Routines
You can describe your daily routine using present tense verbs and reflexive verbs.

> **Me despierto a las siete, desayuno, luego trabajo.** *(I wake up at seven, have breakfast, then work.)*

### Unit 9 — Weather
You can describe the weather using *hace*, *está*, and *hay* expressions, and discuss the seasons.

> **Hace mucho calor hoy. ¿Va a llover?** *(It's very hot today. Is it going to rain?)*

## Key Grammar Summary

| Grammar Point | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Gender agreement | el libro **rojo** / la casa **roja** |
| Reflexive verbs | **Me** despierto / **te** duchas |
| Present tense -ar | yo trabaj**o** / tú trabaj**as** |
| Me gusta / me gustan | Me gust**a** el café / Me gust**an** los tacos |
| Ser vs. Estar | Mi hermana **es** alta / El banco **está** cerca |
| Weather with hacer | **Hace** calor / **Hace** frío |
